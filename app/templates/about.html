About Sahay

Project Documentation & Technical Specifications

Sahay is a web application built to assist in disaster preparedness through ML-powered flood prediction and rainfall analysis. The platform lets users query river water levels for historical dates or forecast up to 12 months ahead, and analyze monthly rainfall patterns across all Indian meteorological subdivisions.

Historical river data — discharge, flood runoff, daily runoff, and weekly runoff — for five major Indian rivers (Cauvery, Godavari, Krishna, Mahanadi, and Son) was used to train ARIMA time-series forecasting models and an LDA flood classifier. Rainfall analysis is powered by a 1D Convolutional Neural Network trained on the IMD Sub-Division dataset (1901–2021).

Flood forecasting requires accurate estimates of the spatial and temporal rainfall rates over a catchment. By integrating both modules, Sahay provides a holistic view of hydrological risk — helping communities and authorities prepare and minimize losses from flooding events.

Technical Approach

Data & Preprocessing

Historical river discharge and runoff data was gathered and preprocessed with mean imputation for missing values. SMOTE oversampling was applied to handle class imbalance in the flood label column.

Flood Forecasting

Real-time ARIMA time-series models (order 5,1,0) are fitted on-the-fly to historical data to dynamically forecast water levels up to 12 months ahead based on user input dates.

LDA Classifier

A Linear Discriminant Analysis classifier was trained on normalised hydrological features and saved as a .pkl file, enabling fast water-level classification (Normal / High) for both historical and future dates.

Rainfall CNN & Random Forest

Users can toggle between a cached 1D-CNN (Deep Learning) or a dynamically trained Random Forest Regressor (Ensemble ML) to predict and compare monthly subdivision rainfall.

AJAX JSON Parsing

The Blood Bank directory parses an 8,000-row dataset into a globally cached Python dictionary, exposing asynchronous JSON endpoints for lightning-fast client-side querying.

Prototype Simulation

Employs dynamic client-side JavaScript to mock up an incident reporting interface, generating randomized geolocation coordinates to simulate emergency dispatches without real alerting.

Core Modules Overview

Flood Prediction Pipeline

Predicts hydrological discharge and classifies flood risk (Normal/High) using historical water level parameters. It performs real-time ARIMA forecasting to project future river metrics up to late 2026, and leverages a trained Linear Discriminant Analysis (LDA) model for accurate state classification.

Rainfall Analysis & Forecasting

Analyzes and forecasts monthly precipitation across all 36 meteorological subdivisions of India. Powered by a robust 1D Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) trained on over a century of historical IMD data (1901–2021). The model leverages local caching to deliver instant predictions.

Blood Assistance Portal

Integrates a dynamic search directory indexing 8,000+ government-approved blood banks across all Indian states and cities. Features live city auto-complete suggestions, click-to-copy numbers, and an interactive physical donor eligibility quiz checker.

Actionable SOP Checklists

Empowers communities with direct, step-by-step preparedness and survival checklists (Do's and Don'ts) covering high-alert disaster domains: floods, landslides, earthquakes, cyclones, tsunamis, wildfires, and thunderstorms.

Emergency Contacts Directory

Maintains a direct public safety communications board providing instant telephone routing for police departments, emergency medical hospitals, fire stations, local NDRF teams, and the PM National Relief Fund.

Incident Reporting Simulator

An experimental prototype coordinate logging tool designed to let community members simulate hazard dispatches, logging fires, landslides, or coastal rising waters locally without real-world rescue agency alerts.
