Standard Operating Procedures

Actionable Preparedness Guidelines & Emergency Checklists

Flood Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Move to Higher Ground: Relocate to elevated surfaces or upper building levels to stay above rising water levels.
  • Follow Evacuations: Obey immediate local responder evacuation instructions and routes without delay.
  • Turn Off Utilities: Disconnect main electrical switches and gas valves to prevent shock and explosions.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Don't Walk Through Water: Flowing water as shallow as 6 inches is enough to knock an adult off their feet.
  • Avoid Flooded Driving: Never drive in flooded areas. Turn around, don't drown! Cars can be swept away easily.
  • Don't Touch Wet Electric: Avoid electrical equipment if standing in water to prevent fatal electrocution.

Earthquake Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Take cover under a heavy desk or sturdy furniture immediately to protect from falling debris.
  • Stay Indoors: Remain inside until the ground stops shaking completely; it is safer than running out during tremors.
  • Provide First Aid: Check yourself and others for injuries once safe and treat them or call for help.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Use Elevators: Power failures risk you being trapped inside elevators. Always use stairwells.
  • Avoid Windows: Stay far away from glass doors, mirrors, and window panels that could shatter violently.
  • Do Not Re-enter Buildings: Stay outside and avoid damaged structures until professionals declare them safe.

Cyclone Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Stay Inside Safe Rooms: Stay in the safest, most interior room away from exterior walls and windows.
  • Secure Loose Objects: Anchor or bring inside outdoor items to prevent them from becoming deadly flying debris.
  • Listen for Warnings: Continuously monitor emergency radios or civic weather updates for path changes.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Venture Out: Do not go outside during the calm eye of the storm; the back wall of the cyclone will hit suddenly.
  • Don't Use Core Lines: Avoid using non-essential electrical equipment during intense lightning and wind.
  • Avoid Road Travel: Severe wind gusts can easily flip or sweep vehicles off roads completely.

Landslide Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Evacuate Path Immediately: Relocate quickly away from the path of sliding rocks and moving earth.
  • Listen for Rumbling: Stay alert for unusual sounds like trees cracking or boulders knocking together.
  • Obey Warning Signs: Follow road closures and localized landslide warnings issued by authorities.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Return Early: Stay away until comprehensive geological surveys declare the zone fully safe.
  • Avoid Steep Slopes: Stay completely away from loose cliffs or newly eroded hillsides prone to secondary slips.
  • Do Not Ignore Shifts: Do not ignore warning signs like shifting foundation tiles or new wall cracking.

Tsunami Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Move Inland Immediately: Relocate to highly elevated terrain or upper levels of reinforced concrete buildings upon hearing any warning.
  • Evacuate on Foot: Evacuate on foot if possible to prevent severe traffic congestion and gridlock on coastal escape routes.
  • Listen to Local Alerts: Monitor emergency broadcasts constantly and immediately follow directives from coastal response forces.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Go to the Beach: Never go to the shore to watch a tsunami. If you can see the wave approaching, you are already too close to escape.
  • Do Not Return Early: Stay clear of impacted zones until state and local authorities issue a formal "All Clear" declaration.
  • Do Not Ignore Natural Warnings: Never ignore ocean drawback where wet sea floor is suddenly exposed; this is a critical immediate trigger to run.

Wildfire Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Evacuate Early: Evacuate immediately if ordered by authorities or if smoke/embers approach your residential area.
  • Seal House Openings: Shut all windows, exterior doors, and vents to stop highly volatile embers from igniting the interior.
  • Filter out Smoke: Wear N95 respirator masks or cover your face with a thick damp cloth to protect your lungs from toxic soot.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Delay Evacuation: Do not wait until the last minute to evacuate; heavy smoke, blinding heat, and fast fire progress can block exit roads.
  • Do Not Block Access Roads: Keep driveways clear and never block access paths needed by heavy fire trucks and response vehicles.
  • Do Not Accumulate Fuel: Do not store firewood, brush piles, or gas canisters within 30 feet of your house walls.

Thunderstorm & Lightning Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Seek Sturdy Indoor Shelter: Go immediately inside a substantial building or fully enclosed, metal-topped vehicle.
  • Unplug Electronics: Disconnect non-essential electrical equipment to prevent severe damage from lightning power surges.
  • Avoid Corded Items: Keep away from landlines, running faucets, showers, and metal piping that conduct lightning into homes.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Stand Under Trees: Never take shelter under isolated trees, high poles, open towers, or light picnic structures.
  • Avoid Water Bodies: Immediately exit and stay clear of swimming pools, lakes, rivers, wet beaches, and open fields.
  • Do Not Lay Flat: If caught in the open, crouch down low with your feet together and heels touching to minimize the electric path.
