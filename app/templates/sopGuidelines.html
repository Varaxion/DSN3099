Standard Operating Procedures

Actionable Preparedness Guidelines & Emergency Checklists

Flood Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Higher GroundRelocate to elevated surfaces or upper building levels.
  • Follow EvacuationsObey immediate local responder evacuation instructions.
  • Turn Off UtilitiesDisconnect main electrical switches and gas valves.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Walk Through WaterFlowing water as shallow as 6 inches can knock you over.
  • Avoid Flooded DrivingNever drive in flooded areas. Turn around, don't drown!
  • Don't Touch Wet ElectricAvoid electrical equipment if standing in water.

Earthquake Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold OnTake cover under heavy furniture immediately.
  • Stay IndoorsRemain inside until the ground stops shaking.
  • Provide First AidCheck yourself and others for injuries and treat them.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Use ElevatorsPower failure risks elevator trapping. Use stairwells.
  • Avoid WindowsStay away from glass doors, mirrors, and window panels.
  • Do Not Re-enter BuildingsStay outside and avoid damaged structures until checked.

Cyclone Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Stay InsideStay in safe rooms away from exterior walls and windows.
  • Secure Loose ObjectsAnchor outdoor items to prevent flying debris.
  • Listen for WarningsMonitor emergency radios or civic weather updates.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Venture OutDo not go outside during the calm eye of the storm.
  • Don't Use Core LinesAvoid using non-essential electrical equipment.
  • Avoid Road TravelWind gusts can easily sweep vehicles off roads.

Landslide Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Evacuate PathRelocate quickly away from sliding rock paths.
  • Listen for RumblingStay alert for unusual sounds or ground crackling.
  • Obey Warning SignsFollow road closures and localized landslide warnings.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Return EarlyStay away until geological surveys declare it safe.
  • Avoid Steep SlopesStay away from loose cliffs or newly eroded hillsides.
  • Do Not Ignore Ground ShiftDo not ignore shifting tiles or wall cracking.

Tsunami Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Move Inland ImmediatelyRelocate to elevated terrain or upper levels of reinforced concrete buildings upon hearing a warning.
  • Evacuate on FootEvacuate on foot if possible to prevent severe traffic congestion and gridlock on coastal escape routes.
  • Listen to Local AlertsMonitor emergency broadcasts and immediately follow directives from coastal response forces.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Go to the BeachNever go to the shore to watch a tsunami. If you can see it approaching, you are already too close to escape.
  • Do Not Return EarlyStay clear of impacted zones until state and local authorities issue a formal "All Clear" declaration.
  • Do Not Ignore Natural WarningsNever ignore ocean drawback where wet sea floor is suddenly exposed; this is a critical immediate trigger to run.

Wildfire Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Evacuate EarlyEvacuate immediately if ordered or if smoke/embers approach your residential area.
  • Seal House OpeningsShut all windows, exterior doors, and vents to stop highly volatile embers from igniting the interior.
  • Filter out SmokeWear N95 respirator masks or cover your face with a thick damp cloth to protect your lungs from toxic soot.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Delay EvacuationDo not wait until the last minute to evacuate; heavy smoke, blinding heat, and fire progress can block exit roads.
  • Do Not Block Access RoadsKeep driveways clear and never block access paths needed by heavy fire trucks and response vehicles.
  • Do Not Accumulate FuelDo not store firewood, brush piles, or gas canisters within 30 feet of your house walls.

Thunderstorm & Lightning Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Seek Sturdy Indoor ShelterGo immediately inside a substantial building or fully enclosed, metal-topped vehicle.
  • Unplug ElectronicsDisconnect non-essential electrical equipment to prevent severe damage from power surges.
  • Avoid Corded ItemsKeep away from landlines, running faucets, showers, and metal piping that conduct lightning.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Stand Under TreesNever take shelter under isolated trees, high poles, open towers, or light picnic structures.
  • Avoid Water BodiesImmediately exit and stay clear of swimming pools, lakes, rivers, wet beaches, and open fields.
  • Do Not Lay FlatIf caught in the open, crouch down low with your feet together and heels touching to minimize electric path.
