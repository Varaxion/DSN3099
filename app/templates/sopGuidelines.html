Standard Operating Procedures

Actionable Preparedness Guidelines & Emergency Checklists

Flood Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Higher Ground: Relocate to elevated surfaces or upper building levels.
  • Follow Evacuations: Obey immediate local responder evacuation instructions.
  • Turn Off Utilities: Disconnect main electrical switches and gas valves.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Walk Through Water: Flowing water as shallow as 6 inches can knock you over.
  • Avoid Flooded Driving: Never drive in flooded areas. Turn around, don't drown!
  • Don't Touch Wet Electric: Avoid electrical equipment if standing in water.

Earthquake Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On: Take cover under heavy furniture immediately.
  • Stay Indoors: Remain inside until the ground stops shaking.
  • Provide First Aid: Check yourself and others for injuries and treat them.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Use Elevators: Power failure risks elevator trapping. Use stairwells.
  • Avoid Windows: Stay away from glass doors, mirrors, and window panels.
  • Do Not Re-enter Buildings: Stay outside and avoid damaged structures until checked.

Cyclone Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Stay Inside: Stay in safe rooms away from exterior walls and windows.
  • Secure Loose Objects: Anchor outdoor items to prevent flying debris.
  • Listen for Warnings: Monitor emergency radios or civic weather updates.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Venture Out: Do not go outside during the calm eye of the storm.
  • Don't Use Core Lines: Avoid using non-essential electrical equipment.
  • Avoid Road Travel: Wind gusts can easily sweep vehicles off roads.

Landslide Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Evacuate Path: Relocate quickly away from sliding rock paths.
  • Listen for Rumbling: Stay alert for unusual sounds or ground crackling.
  • Obey Warning Signs: Follow road closures and localized landslide warnings.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Return Early: Stay away until geological surveys declare it safe.
  • Avoid Steep Slopes: Stay away from loose cliffs or newly eroded hillsides.
  • Do Not Ignore Ground Shift: Do not ignore shifting tiles or wall cracking.

Tsunami Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Move Inland Immediately: Relocate to elevated terrain or upper levels of reinforced concrete buildings upon hearing a warning.
  • Evacuate on Foot: Evacuate on foot if possible to prevent severe traffic congestion and gridlock on coastal escape routes.
  • Listen to Local Alerts: Monitor emergency broadcasts and immediately follow directives from coastal response forces.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Go to the Beach: Never go to the shore to watch a tsunami. If you can see it approaching, you are already too close to escape.
  • Do Not Return Early: Stay clear of impacted zones until state and local authorities issue a formal "All Clear" declaration.
  • Do Not Ignore Natural Warnings: Never ignore ocean drawback where wet sea floor is suddenly exposed; this is a critical immediate trigger to run.

Wildfire Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Evacuate Early: Evacuate immediately if ordered or if smoke/embers approach your residential area.
  • Seal House Openings: Shut all windows, exterior doors, and vents to stop highly volatile embers from igniting the interior.
  • Filter out Smoke: Wear N95 respirator masks or cover your face with a thick damp cloth to protect your lungs from toxic soot.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Delay Evacuation: Do not wait until the last minute to evacuate; heavy smoke, blinding heat, and fire progress can block exit roads.
  • Do Not Block Access Roads: Keep driveways clear and never block access paths needed by heavy fire trucks and response vehicles.
  • Do Not Accumulate Fuel: Do not store firewood, brush piles, or gas canisters within 30 feet of your house walls.

Thunderstorm & Lightning Guidelines

Recommended Actions (Do's)

  • Seek Sturdy Indoor Shelter: Go immediately inside a substantial building or fully enclosed, metal-topped vehicle.
  • Unplug Electronics: Disconnect non-essential electrical equipment to prevent severe damage from power surges.
  • Avoid Corded Items: Keep away from landlines, running faucets, showers, and metal piping that conduct lightning.

Actions to Avoid (Don'ts)

  • Do Not Stand Under Trees: Never take shelter under isolated trees, high poles, open towers, or light picnic structures.
  • Avoid Water Bodies: Immediately exit and stay clear of swimming pools, lakes, rivers, wet beaches, and open fields.
  • Do Not Lay Flat: If caught in the open, crouch down low with your feet together and heels touching to minimize electric path.
